## 1. Types of Assurance Services

### The Three Main Service Types

| Service | Level | Opinion/Report |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| **Audit** | Highest | Opinion on fair presentation |
| **Review** | Limited | No material modifications identified |
| **Compilation** | None | No assurance provided |

---

### Audit Engagement

**Definition:** An examination of financial statements to provide reasonable assurance that they are free from material misstatement.

**Key Features:**
- Highest level of assurance
- Requires evidence gathering
- Testing of internal controls
- Substantive testing of balances
- Issues an opinion

**Output:** Audit Report with Opinion

---

### Review Engagement

**Definition:** A limited assurance engagement consisting primarily of inquiries and analytical procedures.

**Key Features:**
- Limited assurance
- Primarily inquiry and analytics
- No testing of controls
- No detailed substantive testing
- Less extensive than audit

**Output:** Review Report (Limited Assurance)

---

### Compilation Engagement

**Definition:** Presenting financial information in the form of financial statements without providing any assurance.

**Key Features:**
- No assurance provided
- Accountant assists in preparing statements
- No testing performed
- Reading for obvious errors only
- Management responsible for content

**Output:** Compilation Report (No Assurance)

---

### What "Assurance" Means

| Level | Meaning | Risk |
|-------|---------|------|
| **Reasonable (Audit)** | High confidence statements are fairly presented | Low but not zero |
| **Limited (Review)** | No evidence of material misstatement | Moderate |
| **None (Compilation)** | No assurance - rely on management | High |

## 3. When Each Service is Required

### Audit Required

| Situation | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Bank/Lender Requirement** | Loan covenant |
| **Surety/Bonding** | Required for large bonds |
| **Government Contracts** | Single Audit if federal funds |
| **Contract Requirement** | Owner/GC requirement |
| **Ownership Agreements** | Buy-sell, partnership agreements |
| **Size Threshold** | Some states require by size |

---

### Review Sufficient

| Situation | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Bank/Lender** | Sometimes acceptable for smaller loans |
| **Surety** | May accept for smaller programs |
| **SBA Loans** | Often acceptable |
| **Internal Use** | Management reporting |

---

### Compilation Acceptable

| Situation | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Small Contractors** | Limited external requirements |
| **Tax Basis Statements** | For tax return support |
| **Internal Management** | Basic financial reporting |
| **Start-ups** | Building financial history |

---

### Surety Bonding Requirements

| Bond Program Size | Typical Requirement |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Under $5M | Compilation or Review |
| $5M - $15M | Review or Audit |
| $15M - $50M | Audit usually required |
| Over $50M | Audit required |

*Requirements vary by surety and contractor risk profile*

## 4. Professional Standards

### Audit Standards

| Standard | Source | Applicability |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| **GAAS** | AICPA | Non-issuers (private companies) |
| **PCAOB** | PCAOB | Public companies |
| **GAGAS** | GAO | Government audits |
| **Single Audit** | OMB | Federal funds recipients |

**Key GAAS Statements:**
- AU-C Section 200: Overall Objectives
- AU-C Section 315: Understanding Entity and Environment
- AU-C Section 330: Responses to Assessed Risks
- AU-C Section 500: Audit Evidence
- AU-C Section 700: Forming an Opinion

---

### Review Standards

| Standard | Source |
|----------|--------|
| **AR-C Section 90** | AICPA SSARS |

**Key Requirements:**
- Understanding of industry
- Understanding of entity
- Analytical procedures
- Inquiries of management
- Reading financial statements

---

### Compilation Standards

| Standard | Source |
|----------|--------|
| **AR-C Section 80** | AICPA SSARS |

**Key Requirements:**
- Understanding sufficient to compile
- Reading for obvious errors
- No requirement to verify information
- Can compile without independence (with disclosure)

---

### Report Comparison

#### Audit Report - Key Language:
```
"In our opinion, the accompanying financial statements present fairly,
in all material respects, the financial position of ABC Construction,
Inc. as of December 31, 2024, and the results of its operations and
its cash flows for the year then ended in accordance with accounting
principles generally accepted in the United States of America."
```

#### Review Report - Key Language:
```
"Based on our review, we are not aware of any material modifications
that should be made to the accompanying financial statements in order
for them to be in accordance with accounting principles generally
accepted in the United States of America."
```

#### Compilation Report - Key Language:
```
"We did not audit or review the financial statements nor were we
required to perform any procedures to verify the accuracy or
completeness of the information provided by management. We do not
express an opinion, a conclusion, nor provide any form of assurance
on these financial statements."
```

## 6. Construction-Specific Considerations

### High-Risk Areas for All Services

| Area | Risk Level | Why |
|------|------------|-----|
| **WIP/Revenue Recognition** | HIGH | Estimates, judgment |
| **Cost to Complete Estimates** | HIGH | Management bias possible |
| **Retention Receivable** | MEDIUM | Collectibility |
| **Related Party Transactions** | MEDIUM | Proper disclosure |
| **Loss Contracts** | HIGH | Immediate recognition |
| **Change Orders/Claims** | HIGH | Variable consideration |

---

### Audit Emphasis for Construction

**Primary Focus Areas:**
1. WIP schedule testing
2. Job cost verification
3. Estimate accuracy (ETC)
4. Cutoff testing
5. Contract review
6. Over/under billing verification

---

### Review Emphasis for Construction

**Primary Inquiry Areas:**
1. How are estimates to complete determined?
2. Have there been significant cost overruns?
3. Are there any loss contracts?
4. Are there unapproved change orders or claims?
5. Any contract disputes?
6. Related party relationships?

**Primary Analytics:**
1. Gross profit trend by job
2. Over/under billing reasonableness
3. Backlog analysis
4. Cost category comparisons

---

### Compilation Considerations for Construction

**Even with No Assurance, Accountant Should:**
1. Understand WIP calculations
2. Review for obvious errors in formulas
3. Consider if presentation is appropriate
4. Note obvious unusual items

---

### Independence Considerations

| Service | Independence Required? |
|---------|----------------------|
| Audit | Yes - absolutely |
| Review | Yes - absolutely |
| Compilation | No - but must disclose lack |

**Common Independence Threats in Construction:**
- Bookkeeping services
- Bank reconciliations
- Payroll processing
- Tax preparation (generally OK)
- Management functions
