# Contract Balances Disclosures

## Overview

Contract balance disclosures are critical for construction companies because significant judgment is involved in recognizing revenue over time. These disclosures help users understand the relationship between billings, costs, and revenue recognition.

## Claims and Unapproved Change Orders

### Template: Detailed Claims Disclosure

**Claims and Unapproved Change Orders**

The Company's contract assets include amounts related to unapproved change orders and claims as follows:

|  | [DATE] | [PRIOR YEAR DATE] |
|--|--------|-------------------|
| Unapproved change orders | $[AMOUNT] | $[AMOUNT] |
| Contract claims | [AMOUNT] | [AMOUNT] |
| **Total** | **$[AMOUNT]** | **$[AMOUNT]** |

*Unapproved Change Orders*

Unapproved change orders represent additional billings for work performed due to changes in contract specifications or scope. The Company includes unapproved change orders in the transaction price when it is probable that they will be approved and the amount can be reliably estimated. The Company's assessment considers historical experience, documentation, and negotiations with customers.

*Claims*

Claims represent amounts in excess of agreed contract prices that the Company seeks to collect from customers or third parties for delays, errors in specifications, or other causes of unanticipated additional costs. Claims are included in revenue when it is probable that the claim will result in additional contract revenue, the amount can be reliably estimated, and there is a legal basis for the claim.

The Company currently has claims pending resolution on [XX] contracts. While the Company believes its claims are valid and collectible, the ultimate resolution of claims may result in amounts that differ from recorded amounts.
