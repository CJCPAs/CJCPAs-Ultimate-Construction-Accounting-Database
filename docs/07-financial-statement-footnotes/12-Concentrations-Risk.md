# Concentrations and Risk Disclosures

## Overview

ASC 275, Risks and Uncertainties, and ASC 825, Financial Instruments, require disclosure of significant concentrations of credit risk, customer concentrations, and other risk factors. Construction companies often have significant concentrations due to the project-based nature of the business.

---

## Significant Estimates and Uncertainties

### Template: Estimates Disclosure

**Significant Estimates and Uncertainties**

The Company's financial statements include estimates that are particularly sensitive to changes in conditions:

*Contract Revenue and Cost Estimates*
Revenue is recognized using the cost-to-cost method, which requires estimates of total contract costs. These estimates may be revised as conditions change. Significant changes in estimates could result in material adjustments to revenue and profitability.

*Accounts Receivable Collectibility*
The allowance for doubtful accounts requires judgment about the collectibility of receivables. Economic downturns affecting the Company's customers could result in increased uncollectible accounts.

*Self-Insurance Reserves*
The Company self-insures portions of its workers' compensation, general liability, and health insurance programs. Reserves for claims are based on historical experience and actuarial estimates, which are subject to variability.

*Legal Contingencies*
The Company is involved in various legal matters. The ultimate outcomes of these matters are uncertain and could differ materially from amounts accrued.
