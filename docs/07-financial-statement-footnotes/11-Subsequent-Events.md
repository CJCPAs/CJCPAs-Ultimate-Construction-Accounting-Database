# Subsequent Events Disclosures

## Overview

ASC 855, Subsequent Events, requires entities to evaluate events occurring after the balance sheet date through the date the financial statements are issued (or available to be issued) and to disclose the date through which subsequent events were evaluated.

---

## Types of Subsequent Events

### Recognized Events (Type I)
Events that provide additional evidence about conditions that existed at the balance sheet date. These events require adjustment to the financial statements.

### Nonrecognized Events (Type II)
Events that provide evidence about conditions that arose after the balance sheet date. These events require disclosure but not adjustment.

---

## Standard Subsequent Events Disclosure

### Template 1: No Significant Subsequent Events

**Subsequent Events**

The Company has evaluated subsequent events through [DATE], the date the financial statements were [issued | available to be issued]. No events requiring recognition or disclosure in the financial statements were identified.

## Recognized Subsequent Events (Type I)

### Template 1: Collection of Receivable

**Subsequent Events - Receivable Collection**

Subsequent to year-end, the Company collected $[AMOUNT] on an account receivable that was fully reserved at [DATE]. The allowance for doubtful accounts has been adjusted to reflect this collection.

---

### Template 2: Settlement of Litigation

**Subsequent Events - Litigation Settlement**

On [DATE], subsequent to year-end, the Company settled litigation with [PLAINTIFF/DEFENDANT] for $[AMOUNT]. This settlement was within the range of the Company's estimate at [DATE], and accordingly, the accrued liability at year-end adequately covers the settlement amount.

{OR, if different from estimate:}

On [DATE], subsequent to year-end, the Company settled litigation with [PLAINTIFF/DEFENDANT] for $[AMOUNT]. This settlement [exceeded | was less than] the amount accrued at [DATE] by $[AMOUNT]. The financial statements have been adjusted to reflect the actual settlement amount.

---

### Template 3: Contract Loss Identification

**Subsequent Events - Contract Loss**

Subsequent to year-end, the Company received revised cost estimates on [PROJECT NAME] indicating that the contract will result in a loss at completion. The estimated loss of $[AMOUNT] has been recognized in the accompanying financial statements as of [DATE].

---

### Template 4: Customer Bankruptcy

**Subsequent Events - Customer Bankruptcy**

On [DATE], subsequent to year-end, [CUSTOMER NAME], a customer of the Company, filed for bankruptcy protection. At [DATE], the Company had accounts receivable from this customer of $[AMOUNT]. Based on information available subsequent to year-end, the Company has increased its allowance for doubtful accounts by $[AMOUNT] to reflect the estimated uncollectible amount.

---

### Template 5: Asset Impairment

**Subsequent Events - Asset Impairment**

Subsequent to year-end, the Company determined that certain [equipment | assets] were impaired based on events that existed at [DATE]. An impairment charge of $[AMOUNT] has been recognized in the accompanying financial statements.

---

## Nonrecognized Subsequent Events (Type II)

### Template 1: New Debt or Financing

**Subsequent Events - Financing**

On [DATE], subsequent to year-end, the Company [obtained new financing | refinanced existing debt] as follows:

*New Credit Facility:*
The Company entered into a new credit agreement with [BANK NAME] providing for a $[AMOUNT] revolving credit facility and a $[AMOUNT] term loan. The new facility replaces the Company's prior credit agreement and provides [more favorable terms | additional capacity | extended maturity].

*Refinancing:*
The Company refinanced its existing debt with [BANK NAME], resulting in [reduced interest rates | extended maturity | modified covenants]. The refinancing [will | will not] result in a gain or loss on extinguishment of debt.

### Template 5: Contract Termination or Cancellation

**Subsequent Events - Contract Termination**

On [DATE], subsequent to year-end, [OWNER NAME] terminated the contract for [PROJECT NAME] for [convenience | cause | other reason]. The contract had a remaining value of approximately $[AMOUNT] at the date of termination.

{IF TERMINATED FOR CAUSE:}
The Company disputes the termination and intends to pursue all available remedies under the contract. The Company does not expect to incur material losses as a result of this termination.

{IF TERMINATED FOR CONVENIENCE:}
The Company expects to recover its costs incurred plus a reasonable profit under the termination for convenience provisions of the contract.

**Subsequent Events - Casualty Loss**

On [DATE], subsequent to year-end, the Company experienced [fire | flood | hurricane | theft | other casualty] at [LOCATION]. The estimated loss is approximately $[AMOUNT], of which $[AMOUNT] is expected to be recovered through insurance, subject to the policy deductible of $[AMOUNT].

### Template 7: Litigation Filed

**Subsequent Events - Litigation**

The Company believes it has meritorious defenses and intends to vigorously defend this action. It is too early to predict the outcome or estimate potential losses, if any.

### Template 8: Litigation Resolved

**Subsequent Events - Litigation Resolution**

This resolution was [within | outside] the range estimated at year-end. {IF DIFFERENT:} The Company expects to recognize a [charge | credit] of approximately $[AMOUNT] in the [first quarter | subsequent period] related to this resolution.

## Economic/Market Subsequent Events

**Subsequent Events - Economic Conditions**

The Company is monitoring these conditions and their potential impact on operations.
