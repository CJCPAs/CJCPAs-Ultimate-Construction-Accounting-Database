# Joint Ventures and Investments Disclosures

## Overview

Construction companies frequently participate in joint ventures and other investment arrangements to pursue large projects, share risk, or expand capabilities. These disclosures cover equity method investments, joint venture arrangements, and variable interest entities (VIEs).

## Joint Venture Accounting

### Template 1: Construction Joint Venture - General

**Construction Joint Ventures**

The Company participates in construction joint ventures to pursue large or complex projects that exceed the Company's capacity or risk tolerance for individual pursuit. Joint venture arrangements typically provide for:

- Shared capital contributions based on ownership percentages
- Joint and several liability on bonded projects
- Allocation of management responsibilities among joint venture partners
- Distribution of profits in accordance with the joint venture agreement

The Company's joint ventures are typically structured as [partnerships | limited liability companies | unincorporated joint ventures] and are accounted for under the [equity method | proportionate consolidation for unincorporated JVs in certain circumstances].

- Its share of expenses of the joint operations
- Revenue from sale of its output from the joint operation
- Expenses incurred directly by the Company

For the year ended [DATE], the Company recognized revenue of $[AMOUNT] and costs of $[AMOUNT] from jointly controlled operations.

---

### Template 3: Joint Venture Risk Disclosure

**Joint Venture Risk Factors**

The Company's participation in joint ventures involves certain risks:

*Joint and Several Liability*
On bonded joint venture projects, each partner is typically jointly and severally liable for the performance of the entire project. If a joint venture partner fails to perform, the Company may be required to complete the partner's portion of the work, potentially without additional compensation.

*Capital Calls*
Joint venture agreements typically provide for additional capital contributions if the joint venture requires funding. The Company may be obligated to fund its proportionate share of capital calls.

*Profit/Loss Allocation*
The allocation of joint venture profits and losses may differ from ownership percentages based on factors such as performance, responsibility for certain work, or other provisions in the joint venture agreement.
