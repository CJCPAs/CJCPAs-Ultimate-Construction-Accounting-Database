# Going Concern Disclosures

## Overview

ASC 205-40, Going Concern, requires management to evaluate whether there are conditions or events that raise substantial doubt about an entity's ability to continue as a going concern within one year after the financial statements are issued. These disclosures are particularly important for construction companies facing financial difficulties.

---

## Going Concern Assessment Framework

### Management's Evaluation

Management is required to evaluate:
1. Whether conditions or events raise substantial doubt about the entity's ability to continue as a going concern
2. Whether management's plans alleviate the substantial doubt
3. The nature of required disclosures based on the assessment

### Substantial Doubt Indicators

Common indicators in construction companies:
- Recurring operating losses
- Working capital deficiencies
- Negative cash flows from operations
- Loan covenant violations
- Loss of bonding capacity
- Loss of major customers or contracts
- Litigation with significant potential liability
- Labor disputes or skilled labor shortages
- Inability to pay obligations as they come due

## Substantial Doubt - Alleviated by Management's Plans

### Template 1: Financial Restructuring

**Going Concern**

The accompanying financial statements have been prepared on a going concern basis, which contemplates the realization of assets and satisfaction of liabilities in the normal course of business.

As of [DATE], the Company had [negative working capital of $[AMOUNT] | an accumulated deficit of $[AMOUNT] | recurring operating losses]. These conditions raised substantial doubt about the Company's ability to continue as a going concern.

*Management's Plans*

Management has implemented the following plans to address these conditions:

1. **Debt Restructuring**: On [DATE], the Company entered into an amendment to its credit agreement that [extended maturity | reduced principal payments | modified covenants | provided additional borrowing capacity].

2. **Cost Reduction**: The Company has implemented cost reduction measures expected to generate annual savings of approximately $[AMOUNT], including [workforce reductions | facility consolidations | overhead reductions | renegotiated vendor contracts].

3. **Capital Infusion**: [Shareholders | Members | Partners] have committed to contribute additional capital of $[AMOUNT] as needed to support operations.

4. **Asset Sales**: The Company has identified [equipment | real estate | other assets] with expected proceeds of approximately $[AMOUNT] for sale to generate cash.

Based on these plans and management's evaluation, the Company believes it will have sufficient cash flows from operations and available financing to meet its obligations as they become due for at least one year from the date of these financial statements. Accordingly, management believes substantial doubt about the Company's ability to continue as a going concern has been alleviated.

## Substantial Doubt - Not Alleviated

### Template 1: Substantial Doubt Exists

**Going Concern**

The accompanying financial statements have been prepared assuming the Company will continue as a going concern. As discussed below, certain conditions exist that raise substantial doubt about the Company's ability to continue as a going concern.

*Conditions*

- The Company incurred a net loss of $[AMOUNT] for the year ended [DATE]
- The Company has negative working capital of $[AMOUNT] at [DATE]
- The Company has an accumulated deficit of $[AMOUNT]
- The Company is not in compliance with certain debt covenants
- The Company's bonding capacity has been reduced/eliminated

*Management's Plans*

Management is pursuing the following strategies to address these conditions:

1. [PLAN 1 - e.g., Negotiating with lenders for covenant waivers and loan restructuring]
2. [PLAN 2 - e.g., Seeking additional equity investment]
3. [PLAN 3 - e.g., Implementing cost reduction measures]
4. [PLAN 4 - e.g., Selling non-essential assets]

However, there can be no assurance that management will be successful in implementing these plans. The financial statements do not include any adjustments that might result from the outcome of this uncertainty.

### Template 2: Major Contract Loss

**Going Concern**

In [MONTH YEAR], [CUSTOMER NAME] terminated the Company's contract for [PROJECT NAME] for alleged cause. The contract represented approximately [XX]% of the Company's revenue and the Company's largest project. The termination resulted in:

- Loss of approximately $[AMOUNT] in projected revenue
- Write-off of contract assets of $[AMOUNT]
- Potential claims and counterclaims related to the termination

These conditions raise substantial doubt about the Company's ability to continue as a going concern.

### Template 3: Significant Litigation

**Going Concern**

The Company is a defendant in litigation seeking damages of $[AMOUNT], as described in Note [X]. An unfavorable outcome could materially impair the Company's ability to meet its obligations as they become due.

This potential liability, combined with [other adverse conditions], raises substantial doubt about the Company's ability to continue as a going concern.

The Company is vigorously defending this matter and believes it has meritorious defenses. However, the ultimate outcome is uncertain, and an unfavorable result could have a material adverse effect on the Company's financial condition.

## Financial Statement Adjustments

### Template: Adjustments If Liquidation

**NOTE: If liquidation basis accounting is appropriate:**

**Change in Basis of Accounting**

Effective [DATE], the Company changed its basis of accounting from the going concern basis to the liquidation basis of accounting. This change was made because [management decided to liquidate the Company | creditors forced the Company into liquidation | other reason].

Under the liquidation basis:
- Assets are measured at the amount of cash expected to be received in liquidation
- Liabilities include all expected costs of liquidation
- The Company no longer recognizes revenue or expenses from operations
