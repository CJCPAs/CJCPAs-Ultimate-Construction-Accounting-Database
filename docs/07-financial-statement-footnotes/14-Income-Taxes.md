# Income Tax Disclosures

## Overview

Income tax disclosures vary significantly based on the tax status of the entity. This section provides templates for C Corporations (subject to entity-level tax), S Corporations, LLCs, and partnerships (pass-through entities).

## S Corporation Tax Disclosures

### Template 1: S Corporation - Standard

**Income Taxes**

The Company has elected to be treated as an S Corporation under the provisions of the Internal Revenue Code and applicable state laws. Under these provisions, the Company does not pay federal corporate income taxes on its taxable income. Instead, the shareholders are liable for individual income taxes on their proportionate share of the Company's taxable income.

Accordingly, no provision for federal income taxes is included in the accompanying financial statements.

{IF STATE TAXES APPLY:}
The Company is subject to income taxes in certain states that do not recognize S Corporation status or that impose entity-level taxes on S Corporations. State income tax expense was $[AMOUNT] and $[AMOUNT] for the years ended [DATE] and [PRIOR YEAR DATE], respectively.
