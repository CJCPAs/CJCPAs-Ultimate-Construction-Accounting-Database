# Key Inquiries for Construction Reviews

## Overview

Inquiries are a primary procedure in review engagements. For construction companies, inquiries must address industry-specific risks and complexities.

## Inquiry Categories

### Management and Governance

```
GOVERNANCE INQUIRIES

□ Have there been any changes in ownership or management?
□ Have there been any changes to the organizational structure?
□ Are there any related party transactions?
□ Have there been any disputes with owners, shareholders, or partners?
□ Are there any pending or threatened legal actions?
□ Has the company received any regulatory inquiries?
```

### Accounting Policies

```
ACCOUNTING POLICY INQUIRIES

□ Have there been any changes in accounting policies?
□ Have there been any changes in accounting estimates?
□ What is the basis for revenue recognition?
□ How is percentage of completion calculated?
□ How are overhead costs allocated to jobs?
□ What is the policy for recognizing change orders?
□ What is the policy for recognizing claims?
□ How are loss contracts identified and recorded?
```

## Contract-Specific Inquiries

### Revenue Recognition

```
REVENUE RECOGNITION INQUIRIES

□ What method do you use to recognize revenue on long-term contracts?
□ How do you measure progress toward completion?
□ Who is responsible for preparing cost estimates?
□ How often are estimates updated?
□ Have there been any significant estimate revisions this period?
□ What factors have caused estimate changes?
□ How do you determine when a contract should be combined or 
  segmented?
```

### Work in Progress

```
WIP SCHEDULE INQUIRIES

□ Who prepares the WIP schedule?
□ Who reviews and approves the WIP schedule?
□ How often is the WIP schedule updated?
□ Are all active contracts included?
□ How do you identify completed contracts?
□ Are there any inactive or suspended contracts?
□ Have there been any significant changes to contract values?
□ What is the process for incorporating change orders?
```

### Change Orders and Claims

```
CHANGE ORDER/CLAIMS INQUIRIES

□ What is your policy for including unapproved change orders 
  in revenue?
□ What unapproved change orders are currently outstanding?
□ What is the expected timing and likelihood of approval?
□ Are there any claims against owners?
□ What is the status and expected outcome of pending claims?
□ Have there been any disputes related to change orders?
□ What documentation supports pending change orders and claims?
```

### Loss Contracts

```
LOSS CONTRACT INQUIRIES

□ How do you identify loss contracts?
□ Are there any contracts expected to result in a loss?
□ What factors have contributed to loss situations?
□ Have all anticipated losses been recorded?
□ Are there any contracts at risk of becoming loss contracts?
□ What actions are being taken to mitigate losses?
```

## Financial Condition Inquiries

### Liquidity and Going Concern

```
LIQUIDITY INQUIRIES

□ Are you able to meet obligations as they come due?
□ Have there been any loan covenant violations?
□ Have you experienced any difficulty obtaining financing?
□ Have credit facilities been reduced or cancelled?
□ Are there any significant debts maturing in the next year?
□ Have you had to defer any vendor payments?
□ Are there any significant collection issues?
```

### Bonding

```
BONDING INQUIRIES

□ What is your current bonding capacity?
□ Has your bonding capacity changed during the year?
□ Have there been any bond claims?
□ Have you been required to post additional collateral?
□ Have you experienced any difficulty obtaining bonds?
□ Are there any bonded projects with performance issues?
□ What is your relationship status with your surety?
```

## Operational Inquiries

### Projects and Backlog

```
PROJECT INQUIRIES

□ What significant projects were completed this year?
□ What significant new projects were awarded?
□ Are there any projects with performance issues?
□ Have any projects been suspended or cancelled?
□ What is the current backlog of work?
□ What percentage of backlog is bonded?
□ Are there any projects in dispute?
```

### Subcontractors

```
SUBCONTRACTOR INQUIRIES

□ Have there been any subcontractor defaults?
□ Are there disputes with any subcontractors?
□ Have you withheld payment from any subcontractors?
□ Are there any backcharges outstanding?
□ Have any mechanics liens been filed?
□ Are subcontractor insurance certificates current?
```

### Equipment

```
EQUIPMENT INQUIRIES

□ Have there been any significant equipment purchases?
□ Have there been any equipment disposals?
□ How are internal equipment rates determined?
□ Are equipment rates comparable to market rates?
□ Have there been any impairments of equipment?
□ Are there any equipment purchase commitments?
```

## Related Party Inquiries

```
RELATED PARTY INQUIRIES

□ Are there transactions with shareholders, officers, or directors?
□ Are there transactions with affiliated entities?
□ Are there any joint ventures or partnerships?
□ Have related party transactions been at arm's length?
□ Have all related party transactions been identified?
□ Are there any loans to/from related parties?
□ Have there been any guarantees for related parties?
```

## Subsequent Events Inquiries

```
SUBSEQUENT EVENTS INQUIRIES

□ Have any significant contracts been awarded or cancelled?
□ Have there been any loss events on contracts?
□ Have there been any significant estimate revisions?
□ Have any disputes or litigation arisen?
□ Have there been any changes in bonding status?
□ Have there been any loan defaults or covenant violations?
□ Have there been any significant asset purchases or sales?
□ Have there been any ownership changes?
```

## Documentation of Inquiries

### Inquiry Log Template

```
INQUIRY DOCUMENTATION

Date of Inquiry: February 15, 2025
Person Interviewed: Jane Smith, CFO
Interviewer: John Doe, CPA

Topic: Revenue Recognition and WIP

Questions Asked and Responses:

Q1: What method is used to recognize revenue?
A1: "We use the percentage of completion method based on 
    costs incurred divided by total estimated costs."

Q2: How often are estimates updated?
A2: "Monthly. Project managers prepare updates and the CFO 
    reviews all significant changes."

Q3: Have there been significant estimate changes this year?
A3: "Yes, on project 2024-003, we increased the estimate to 
    complete by $200,000 due to unforeseen site conditions. 
    The change was approved by the surety."

Q4: Are there any loss contracts?
A4: "No contracts are currently expected to result in a loss. 
    All projects have positive margins."

[Additional questions and responses...]

Conclusion: Responses appear reasonable and consistent with 
our understanding of the business. No matters requiring 
additional procedures identified.
```

## Evaluating Responses

### Assessment Criteria

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Consistency | Does response match other evidence? |
| Reasonableness | Is the explanation plausible? |
| Completeness | Has the question been fully answered? |
| Knowledge | Does respondent have appropriate knowledge? |
| Evasiveness | Are there signs of reluctance to respond? |

### Response Red Flags

Warning signs in management responses:
- Vague or evasive answers
- Inconsistency with other information
- Unexpected delays in providing information
- Changes in story when follow-up questions asked
- Deflecting questions to others
- Excessive qualifications in responses
