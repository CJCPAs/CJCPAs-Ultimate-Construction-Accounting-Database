## Overview: Construction Company Audit

### Why Construction Audits Are Complex

| Factor | Audit Challenge |
|--------|-----------------|
| Revenue Recognition | Over-time recognition requires estimates |
| Estimates | ETC, % complete are judgment-based |
| Project Accounting | Multiple jobs, complex allocations |
| Related Parties | Common ownership structures |
| Seasonality | Timing of work affects balances |
| Cash Flow Patterns | Irregular, based on project cycle |

## Materiality for Construction Companies

### Setting Materiality

**Common Bases:**
| Base | Typical % | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Revenues | 0.5% - 1% | Growing contractors |
| Total Assets | 1% - 2% | Asset-heavy contractors |
| Net Income | 5% - 10% | Profitable contractors |
| Equity | 2% - 5% | Established contractors |

**Example:**
- Revenue: $15,000,000
- Materiality @ 0.75%: $112,500
- Performance Materiality @ 75%: $84,375
- Trivial threshold @ 5%: $5,625

## Understanding the WIP Schedule Audit

### The Most Critical Audit Area

**Why WIP Testing is Essential:**
1. Drives revenue recognition
2. Affects balance sheet (assets and liabilities)
3. Based on management estimates
4. Significant judgment involved
5. High inherent risk

**See [Section 07 - WIP Audit Comprehensive](../07-WIP-Audit-Comprehensive/) for complete WIP audit procedures.**

## Common Audit Adjustments in Construction

| Issue | Typical Adjustment |
|-------|-------------------|
| ETC understated | Reduce revenue, increase overbillings |
| Unrecorded payables | Increase AP and job costs |
| Revenue cutoff | Adjust billings and revenue |
| Cost cutoff | Adjust job costs |
| Allowance for bad debts | Increase allowance |
| Loss contracts | Record loss provision |
| Related party | Proper classification/disclosure |
